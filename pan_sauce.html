Pan Sauce

A seared piece of meat might sound boring. But when its drizzled with a silky reduction? Always exciting.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (preferrably pan drippings)
  • 1 whole shallot, halved or minced (optional)
  • 1 whole garlic clove, halved or minced (optional)
  • 1/4 of flavourful alcohol
  • 3/4 cup vegetable, beef or chicken stock
  • 2 tablespoons butter (a splash of cream works wonders here)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (optionally adds a nice thickness to the sauce)
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Once youve cleared the pan, add tablespoon of oil and saute the shallots and garlic on medium high heat until fragrant.
  2. Pour in wine or other alcohol and as it simmers, scrape up any and all fond from the bottom of the pan with a spatula.
  3. Reduce the alcohol reduce by about half, roughly three minutes, then pour in the stock and stir into the reduction. Allow the pan to come to a rapid simmer.
  4. Simmer for 3-5 minutes or until reduced by about half or to desired consistency. Pick up and move the pan to gauge how much the sauce is reducing.
  5. Turn the heat down and begin to stir in the butter or cream and continue to stir until completely melted or incorporated.
  6. Enjoy! Pan sauces are at their very best used right away. Drizzle it over your meal or let guests spoon some for themselves.
If your pan sauce reduces too much or it becomes too thick, whisk in a little extra stock to reach a thinner, more pourable consistency.
